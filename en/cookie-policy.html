Cookie Policy

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how aBest.co (“we”, “our”, or “us”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit or interact with our website.

This policy applies to the website: https://www.abest.co

Cookies are small data files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly, improve user experience, and provide certain features.

By continuing to use this website, you acknowledge that cookies may be used in accordance with this Cookie Policy.

Users who do not wish to accept cookies can adjust their browser settings to refuse or delete cookies.

2. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your web browser when you visit a website.

These files contain information that allows the website to recognize your device and remember certain information about your visit.

Cookies may store information such as:

  • language preferences
  • technical settings
  • session identifiers
  • browser settings
  • device type

Cookies do not typically contain information that directly identifies a person. However, in some cases they may be associated with personal data if combined with other information.

Cookies do not contain viruses, malware, or executable code.

3. Why We Use Cookies

Cookies are used on this website for several reasons. These include:

  • ensuring the technical functionality of the website
  • remembering user preferences
  • improving user experience
  • enabling language settings
  • supporting website security
  • supporting geographic content display

Without certain cookies, parts of the website may not function properly.

4. Types of Cookies We Use

Different categories of cookies may be used on this website.

4.1 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are necessary for the basic operation of the website. These cookies allow the website to function properly and enable core functionality such as navigation and access to secure areas.

Examples include:

  • session management
  • security checks
  • language preference storage
  • load balancing

Without essential cookies, the website cannot operate correctly. Because these cookies are strictly necessary, they cannot be disabled through the website interface.

4.2 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies help improve the user experience by remembering user preferences.

These cookies may remember:

  • selected language
  • region settings
  • display preferences
  • user interface settings

For example, when you select a language on our website, a cookie may store this preference so that the same language is used during future visits. These cookies are optional but help make the website more convenient to use.

4.3 Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that are automatically deleted when you close your browser. These cookies allow the website to function during a single browsing session.

Session cookies may be used for:

  • page navigation
  • temporary session identification
  • security checks

They do not remain stored on your device after your session ends.

4.4 Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain stored on your device for a defined period of time. These cookies allow the website to remember certain settings across multiple visits.

Examples include:

  • language preferences
  • regional display settings
  • interface preferences

Persistent cookies automatically expire after a specified duration or can be deleted manually by the user.

5. First-Party and Third-Party Cookies

Cookies can be categorized based on their origin.

First-Party Cookies: Set directly by the website you are visiting. These cookies are used for internal functionality such as remembering language preferences, enabling site functionality, and maintaining session state.

Third-Party Cookies: May be set by external services integrated into the website. These services may include map services, technical infrastructure providers, and embedded content services. Third-party providers may have their own privacy policies and cookie policies.

This website may display maps provided by OpenStreetMap. OpenStreetMap is an open-source mapping service operated by the OpenStreetMap Foundation.

Provider: OpenStreetMap Foundation, St John’s Innovation Centre, Cowley Road, Cambridge, United Kingdom.

When you access pages containing OpenStreetMap maps, your browser may connect to OpenStreetMap servers. This connection may transmit certain technical information, such as IP address, browser information, device type, and operating system.

OpenStreetMap may use technical cookies or similar technologies required for map rendering. More information can be found at: https://www.openstreetmap.org

8. Language and Localization Cookies

This website may store cookies related to language and regional settings. For example, when a visitor selects a language such as English, German, or Turkish, a cookie may be stored to remember the selected language. This ensures that the website displays the preferred language during future visits.

9. Geolocation and Regional Display

In some cases, the website may attempt to determine a visitor’s approximate geographic location using IP-based geolocation technologies. This is used to suggest an appropriate language, display region-relevant information, and optimize user experience.

This geolocation process typically does not store permanent location data. Any location-related information used for language suggestions is generally temporary and not stored as identifiable personal data.

10. Managing Cookies

Users have full control over cookies through their browser settings. Most web browsers allow users to block cookies, delete stored cookies, receive notifications when cookies are set, and limit third-party cookies.

Users can modify cookie settings through their browser preferences. However, disabling cookies may affect certain website functions. For example, language preferences may not be remembered or some features may not operate correctly.
